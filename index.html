Welcome to Turtle Island


Turtle Island Health and Research Society is an indigenous-led Not For Profit Organization based out of Northwest Territories and servicing at-risk citizens across Canada.

With offices in British Columbia, Alberta and Ontario, Turtle Island aims to provide healthcare program support, financial relief and academic subsidy for the betterment of our most impoverished citizens, who live with co-morbid disease states and social disadvantage.

While Indigenous citizens comprise a significant portion of at-risk citizens in canada today, Turtle Island aims to provide relief to all Canadians who find themselves in poverty and living an at-risk lifestyle, specifically in our Urban Centres.

It is the mission of turtle Island and it’s founder James Hiebert to demonstrate that solution to many of our nations problems can be addressed through aboriginal leadership and initiative.

JAMES HIEBERT is a Canadian First Nations Mohawk born in Hay River, Northwest Territories and a former professional hockey player turned entrepreneur.

James Currently is the owner and founder of Texas Solar Systems which has 8 offices across the continent as well as MSS LTD, a medical and surgical supply company based in Canada that services procurement contracts across the country. Growing up in a small at risk native community, James developed a keen interest in making a difference both in the lives of his own family and community but also the lives a Canada’s most impoverished citizens.

After a succesful 8 year professional hockeycareer, James felt it was important to show his community that an aboriginal kid from a small northern town could become a continental entrepreneur and so he began to invest his time and finances into the future: Clean Energy and Healthcare.

Ten years later, James operates a business empire

that totals over $30 Million in annual revenues and is expanding across the continent.

At the same time, James along side his family, hoped to show, once again, that the solutions to some of his own country’s problems could come from an Indigenous source. To that end, he founded the Turtle Island Health and Research Society with the goal of supporting at-risk citizens across Canada through innovative healthcare, financial assistance and research into solutions for their conditions.

As a former hockey player, James believes that only through a team-based approach can great things be achieved and so James and his family have been active in collaborating with Governments, Bands, NFP Societies, Businesses and Universities across the continent in order to bring his vision closer to reality.

James stays close to his roots by hunting, fishing and ocasionally doing a sweat lodge (or two) while raising his two kids.
